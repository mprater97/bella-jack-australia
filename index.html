💜 Bella's Australia

Your life, but upgraded ✨

🎪

Circus & Aerial

Australia has NICA — the National Institute of Circus Arts. It's literally the best circus training centre in the entire country. Aerial silks, rings, trapeze — all there. This is where professional circus performers train. And you could train there too.

🛍️

Shopping

Chadstone is the biggest shopping centre in the Southern Hemisphere. That's not a typo. Plus Chapel Street for indie fashion, Melbourne Central, and Highpoint. And everything's open later than Scotland.

🧋

Bubble Tea & Asian Food

Melbourne is the bubble tea capital of Australia. Gong Cha, CoCo, ShareTea, Tiger Sugar — they're literally everywhere. Plus the best Chinese food outside of China. Dumplings, hotpot, BBQ, yum cha — all within walking distance in Box Hill and the CBD.

🐨

Cute Animals

Koalas. Quokkas. Wombats. Baby kangaroos. You can literally cuddle a koala at wildlife sanctuaries. Healesville Sanctuary is 1 hour from Melbourne — koalas, platypus, echidnas, and more. Plus penguins at Phillip Island. Actual wild penguins.

⛸️

Skating

O'Brien Icehouse in Docklands — massive ice rink right in the city. Plus Oakleigh rink nearby. Ice skating, figure skating, and just hanging out with mates on the ice.

🎵

Music & Culture

Melbourne is Australia's live music capital. More live music venues per person than anywhere else in the world. Street art laneways, festivals, night markets, and a vibe that's completely different from anything in Scotland.

💙 Jack's Australia

Level up your life 🎮

🛹

Skateparks

Australia has some of the best skateparks in the world. And they're free. Frankston skatepark is massive — bowls, rails, ramps. Plus Waterways, Riverslide (right in the city), and dozens more. The weather means you can skate all year round. No more frozen fingers.

🎮

Gaming & VR

Melbourne has Zero Latency — one of the world's first free-roam VR gaming centres. Walk around, shoot zombies, explore space — with your mates. Plus gaming cafes, esports venues, and PAX Australia (the biggest gaming convention in the Southern Hemisphere) happens in Melbourne every year.

⛸️

Ice Hockey

O'Brien Icehouse has a full ice hockey program for juniors. Learn to play, join a team, compete. Plus the Melbourne Ice and Melbourne Mustangs play there — you can watch pro games. Oakleigh rink is even closer for regular skating and hockey training.

🤸

Trampolining & Circus

Bounce Inc — massive indoor trampoline parks with foam pits, dodgeball courts, and freestyle areas. Plus NICA (National Institute of Circus Arts) for proper circus skills — trapeze, tumbling, acrobatics. Way beyond anything in Scotland.

🚲

Biking

Trails everywhere. Lysterfield Mountain Bike Park, You Yangs, and hundreds of km of bike paths along the coast and through parks. Plus the weather means you can ride every single day if you want.

⚡

Pokémon

Melbourne has a huge Pokémon GO community — regular community days in parks, raids everywhere, and way more Pokéstops than Scotland. Plus there are Pokémon card shops and trading communities. And the Royal Botanic Gardens is basically a Pokémon safari zone.

👫 Friends & School

The bit you're worried about. Let's talk about it honestly.

💬

Your Friends Aren't Going Anywhere

This is probably the scariest part. We get it. Your friends matter — a lot. So here's the truth:

  • FaceTime & video calls — you'll see their faces every day, same as now
  • WhatsApp & Snapchat — group chats don't care what country you're in
  • Discord & gaming — Jack, you can still play Minecraft and game with everyone online. The time difference means you'll be gaming when they're just getting home from school
  • They can visit — imagine your best mates coming to stay in Australia. That's the coolest holiday invitation ever
  • You'll visit home — we'll fly back to the UK every year. You'll see everyone

Real friendships survive distance. The ones that matter will still be there.

🏫

New School — Honestly

Starting a new school is nerve-wracking. Here's what actually happens:

  • You'll be the interesting one — you're from Scotland. You have an accent. Everyone will want to talk to you. Seriously.
  • Australian kids are friendly — it's a different vibe. More relaxed, more outdoor, less cliquey
  • Schools have buddy programs — most schools pair new students with someone to show them around for the first few weeks
  • You'll make friends fast through activities — Bella at circus, Jack at skating/hockey. That's where the real friendships form
  • It takes about 4–6 weeks — the first month is weird. Then suddenly you have a group and it feels normal
🕐

The Time Difference

Australia is 9–11 hours ahead of the UK (depending on the time of year). Here's what that actually means:

  • Your morning (7am AU) = their evening before (10pm UK) — say goodnight
  • After school (3:30pm AU) = their morning (6:30am UK) — say good morning
  • Your evening (7pm AU) = their morning (9am UK) — they're at school
  • Weekends — loads of overlap time for calls and gaming

It's not perfect — but it works. Millions of families do it.

🔄

What If I Hate It?

Fair question. Here's the deal:

  • We keep our house in Scotland — it's rented out, not sold. It's always there.
  • If after a year we're not happy — we come home. No drama. We tried something amazing and we came back.
  • But here's what usually happens: the first month is hard. The second month is better. By month three, you can't imagine going back.

This isn't a trap. It's a door. And it opens both ways.
