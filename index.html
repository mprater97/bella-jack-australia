Circus & Aerial

NICA (National Institute of Circus Arts) in Brunswick, Melbourne — the best circus training centre in Australia. Aerial silks, lyra rings, trapeze, tumbling. This is where Cirque du Soleil scouts come to watch. Youth programs from age 8+. Plus Circus Oz runs youth workshops in Collingwood. You could be training where the pros train.

🛍️

Shopping

Chadstone — biggest shopping centre in the Southern Hemisphere. 550+ stores. Zara, H&M, Uniqlo, JD Sports, plus Australian brands like Cotton On and Sportsgirl. Chapel Street in South Yarra for indie fashion and vintage. Melbourne Central with the iconic shot tower. Highpoint in the west. Plus DFO South Wharf for outlet bargains. Late night shopping every Thursday + Friday.

🧋

Bubble Tea & Chinese Food

Melbourne is the bubble tea capital of Australia. Gong Cha, CoCo, ShareTea, Tiger Sugar, Gotcha, T4 — literally on every block in the CBD. Box Hill has an entire Asian food district — dumplings at HuTong, hotpot at Haidilao, BBQ at Gogi, yum cha at Shark Fin House. Chinatown on Little Bourke Street is the oldest in the Southern Hemisphere. Plus Glen Waverley has incredible Korean and Japanese food. You will never run out of places to eat.

🐨

Cute Animals

Healesville Sanctuary (1 hour from Melbourne) — hand-feed kangaroos, see platypus, meet wombats and echidnas. Phillip Island Penguin Parade — every single sunset, hundreds of little penguins waddle up the beach to their burrows. It never gets old. Moonlit Sanctuary — night tours to see possums, owls, and quolls. Rottnest Island (day trip from Perth) — quokka selfies. Plus wild kangaroos at Anglesea Golf Course and koalas in the Great Otway National Park — just sitting in trees by the road.

🎵

Music & Culture

Melbourne is Australia's live music capital — more live venues per capita than anywhere in the world. Hosier Lane and AC/DC Lane for street art. Federation Square for free outdoor events. Queen Victoria Night Market every Wednesday in summer — food, music, shopping under the stars. Moomba Festival, White Night, Melbourne International Comedy Festival. Plus Greville Street in Prahran for record shops and vintage stores. The culture here is next level.

Skateparks

Australia has some of the best skateparks in the world — and they're all free. Frankston Skatepark — huge bowls, street section, rails. Riverslide Skatepark — right on the Yarra River in the city. Waterways Skatepark in Patterson Lakes. Box Hill Skatepark. Geelong Waterfront — massive park right by the beach. Plus Mornington Skatepark if you're near the coast. All year round. No ice. No rain. Just ride.

🎮

Gaming & VR

Zero Latency in North Melbourne — the world's first free-roam VR gaming centre. Walk around a warehouse shooting zombies with your mates. Fortress Melbourne — Australia's biggest esports and gaming venue, right in the CBD. Screens everywhere, gaming PCs, console stations, food. PAX Australia — the biggest gaming convention in the Southern Hemisphere, held in Melbourne every October. Plus VR arcades in Chadstone and Highpoint. And your Minecraft/Xbox? Same account, same worlds, same friends online.

⛸️

Ice Hockey

O'Brien Icehouse in Docklands — Olympic-sized rink, full junior ice hockey program, learn-to-skate, figure skating. Home of the Melbourne Ice and Melbourne Mustangs — watch real pro games. Oakleigh Ice Skating Centre — 15 minutes from Glen Waverley, great for regular sessions and hockey training. Junior leagues run all year. You could be playing competitive hockey within months of arriving.

🤸

Trampolining & Circus

Bounce Inc in Glen Iris and Essendon — massive freestyle areas, foam pits, dodgeball courts, slam dunk zones. Sky Zone in Heatherton. Flip Out in multiple locations. Plus NICA for proper circus skills — trapeze, tumbling, acrobatics, aerial. And Melbourne Gymnastics Centre for competitive trampolining if you want to take it seriously.

🚲

Biking

Lysterfield Mountain Bike Park — 30 min from the city, trails from beginner to expert. You Yangs Regional Park — world-class MTB trails. Forrest Mountain Bike Trails on the Great Ocean Road. Plus hundreds of km of sealed bike paths — Bay Trail along the coast from the city to Frankston, Main Yarra Trail through parkland, Merri Creek Trail. Ride to school, ride to the beach, ride everywhere.

⚡

Pokémon

Melbourne has a massive Pokémon GO community — regular Community Days in Flagstaff Gardens and Royal Botanic Gardens (basically a Pokémon safari zone). Raids everywhere in the CBD. Good Games stores in multiple locations for Pokémon TCG tournaments and trading nights. Gametraders for cards and merch. Plus EB Games and JB Hi-Fi for all gaming gear. The community here is huge and welcoming.

Beach Days

Imagine finishing school on a Friday and driving 10 minutes to Frankston Beach — golden sand, calm water, foreshore cafes. Or Brighton Beach with the iconic colourful bathing boxes. Or Half Moon Bay for snorkelling. Or Mornington for fish and chips on the pier. Weekends at Torquay or Lorne on the Great Ocean Road. This isn't a holiday — it's just life.

🌡️

The Weather

Scotland: 185 rainy days a year. Dark by 3pm in winter. Six layers to leave the house. Melbourne: 230+ sunny days. Winter is 10–15°C (like a Scottish summer). Summer is 25–35°C. The sun sets at 8:30pm in summer. Kids play outside until dark. BBQs in the garden. You'll wonder how you ever lived without it.

🦘

Wildlife

Wild kangaroos at Anglesea and Wilsons Promontory. Koalas in trees along the Great Ocean Road. Penguins at Phillip Island every sunset. Dolphins in Port Phillip Bay — you can swim with them at Sorrento. Rainbow lorikeets and cockatoos in your actual garden. Wombats at Wilsons Prom. Platypus at Healesville. Every weekend is a wildlife adventure.

🏡

Our House

Australian houses are built for living. Open-plan kitchens that flow to outdoor decks. Proper gardens with grass — not a tiny paved yard. Built-in BBQ areas. Many homes have pools. Even a modest 3-bed rental in Frankston or Langwarrin has more space than most UK houses. Kids can play outside. You can actually host people. Room to breathe.

🎄

Christmas on the Beach

Christmas Day is 30°C. Prawns on the BBQ instead of turkey. Swimming at the beach in the morning. Cricket in the garden in the afternoon. Carols by Candlelight at the Sidney Myer Music Bowl on Christmas Eve — 10,000 people singing under the stars. It feels wrong for about 5 minutes. Then it feels like the best Christmas you've ever had.

👫 Friends & School

The bit you're worried about. Let's talk honestly.

Your Friends Aren't Going Anywhere

This is probably the scariest part. We get it. Your friends matter — a lot. So here's the truth:

  • FaceTime & video calls — see their faces every day
  • WhatsApp & Snapchat — group chats don't care what country you're in
  • Discord & gaming — Jack, you can still play Minecraft with everyone. The time difference means you'll be gaming when they're just getting home from school
  • They can visit — imagine your best mates coming to stay in Australia
  • You'll visit home — we fly back to the UK every year

Real friendships survive distance. The ones that matter will still be there.

🏫

New School — Honestly

  • You'll be the interesting one — you're from Scotland. You have an accent. Everyone will want to talk to you.
  • Australian kids are friendly — more relaxed, more outdoor, less cliquey
  • Schools have buddy programs — someone shows you around for the first few weeks
  • Activities = friends fast — Bella at circus, Jack at skating/hockey
  • 4–6 weeks — the first month is weird. Then suddenly you have a group and it feels normal
🔄

What If I Hate It?

  • We keep our house in Scotland — it's rented out, not sold. Always there.
  • If after a year we're not happy — we come home. No drama.
  • But here's what usually happens: the first month is hard. The second is better. By month three, you can't imagine going back.

This isn't a trap. It's a door. And it opens both ways.
