💜 Bella's Australia

Your life, but upgraded ✨

🎪

Circus & Aerial

Australia has NICA — the National Institute of Circus Arts. Aerial silks, rings, trapeze — where professional performers train. And you could train there too.

🛍️

Shopping

Chadstone is the biggest shopping centre in the Southern Hemisphere. Plus Chapel Street for indie fashion, Melbourne Central, and everything's open later than Scotland.

🧋

Bubble Tea & Chinese Food

Melbourne is the bubble tea capital of Australia. Gong Cha, CoCo, ShareTea, Tiger Sugar — everywhere. Plus the best Chinese food outside of China. Dumplings, hotpot, BBQ, yum cha.

🐨

Cute Animals

Koalas. Quokkas. Wombats. Baby kangaroos. You can cuddle a koala at wildlife sanctuaries. Plus penguins at Phillip Island — actual wild penguins parading at sunset.

🎵

Music & Culture

Melbourne is Australia's live music capital. More venues per person than anywhere else. Street art laneways, festivals, night markets, and a vibe completely different from Scotland.

💙 Jack's Australia

Level up your life 🎮

🛹

Skateparks

Australia has some of the best skateparks in the world. And they're free. Frankston skatepark is massive. The weather means you can skate all year round. No more frozen fingers.

🎮

Gaming & VR

Melbourne has Zero Latency — one of the world's first free-roam VR gaming centres. Plus gaming cafes, esports venues, and PAX Australia happens in Melbourne every year.

⛸️

Ice Hockey

O'Brien Icehouse has a full ice hockey program for juniors. Learn to play, join a team, compete. Oakleigh rink is even closer for regular skating and hockey training.

🤸

Trampolining & Circus

Bounce Inc — massive indoor trampoline parks. Plus NICA for proper circus skills — trapeze, tumbling, acrobatics. Way beyond anything in Scotland.

🚲

Biking

Trails everywhere. Lysterfield Mountain Bike Park, You Yangs, and hundreds of km of coastal bike paths. Ride every single day.

⚡

Pokémon

Melbourne has a huge Pokémon GO community. Regular community days, raids everywhere, card shops and trading communities. The Royal Botanic Gardens is basically a Pokémon safari zone.

👫 Friends & School

The bit you're worried about. Let's talk honestly.

💬

Your Friends Aren't Going Anywhere

This is probably the scariest part. We get it. Your friends matter — a lot. So here's the truth:

  • FaceTime & video calls — see their faces every day
  • WhatsApp & Snapchat — group chats don't care what country you're in
  • Discord & gaming — Jack, you can still play Minecraft with everyone. The time difference means you'll be gaming when they're just getting home from school
  • They can visit — imagine your best mates coming to stay in Australia
  • You'll visit home — we fly back to the UK every year

Real friendships survive distance. The ones that matter will still be there.

🏫

New School — Honestly

  • You'll be the interesting one — you're from Scotland. You have an accent. Everyone will want to talk to you.
  • Australian kids are friendly — more relaxed, more outdoor, less cliquey
  • Schools have buddy programs — someone shows you around for the first few weeks
  • Activities = friends fast — Bella at circus, Jack at skating/hockey
  • 4–6 weeks — the first month is weird. Then suddenly you have a group and it feels normal
🔄

What If I Hate It?

  • We keep our house in Scotland — it's rented out, not sold. Always there.
  • If after a year we're not happy — we come home. No drama.
  • But here's what usually happens: the first month is hard. The second is better. By month three, you can't imagine going back.

This isn't a trap. It's a door. And it opens both ways.
